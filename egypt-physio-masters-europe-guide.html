Why a master's?

The study route — advantages, trade-offs & key insight

Choosing to do a master's degree in a European country before practising is a longer and more expensive path than applying for direct recognition — but it can be the smartest move for the right person.

✅ When the master's route makes sense
  • ✓ CORU/TSRM-PSTRP/DREETS required a Period of Adaptation or partial exemption that needs physical presence anyway — a master's gives you a legal visa to be there
  • ✓ You want a specialisation (MSK, neuro, sports, paediatrics) that adds salary and career value
  • ✓ You want to bypass the multi-year direct recognition queue in Italy or France's 2% quota barrier
  • ✓ You plan to stay long-term and want full EU-level credentials
  • ✓ You don't yet speak the local language — a 2-year master's gives you time to learn it properly
  • ✓ Scholarships can offset costs significantly (especially in Belgium and Ireland)
  • ✓ Post-study work rights (12–24 months) give a legal runway to find a job without an employer lined up
⚠️ When direct recognition is better
  • ✗ You already have solid clinical experience and your Egyptian degree closely matches Irish/Belgian standards — direct recognition may be faster and cheaper
  • ✗ You have a family and cannot spend 1–2 years studying abroad on a tight student income
  • ✗ The total cost (€30,000–80,000 over 2 years) is not feasible without scholarship support
  • ✗ You want to start earning physiotherapy income as quickly as possible
💡 The critical insight for CORU (Ireland): If you pursue an Irish CORU-approved pre-registration MSc Physiotherapy (e.g. at UCD or UCC), you graduate as a first-time registrant — you skip the international recognition process entirely. You apply directly to CORU for registration as a new graduate, just like an Irish-trained physio. This is the cleanest, most reliable path to practising in Ireland. However, these programmes are competitive entry (require a science background, NOT for existing physios), so check eligibility carefully. Existing physiotherapists are NOT eligible for pre-registration programmes — their route is the international recognition process OR a postgraduate specialist MSc.
⚠️ Two very different types of master's exist: (1) Pre-registration MSc Physiotherapy — for people who do NOT already have a physio degree; leads to CORU/national registration as a first-time graduate. (2) Postgraduate specialist MSc — for existing qualified physiotherapists; deepens expertise in MSK, sports, neuro, etc., but does NOT replace the recognition process; the Egyptian bachelor's degree is still the qualification assessed for registration. This guide covers both, but highlights which is which for each country.

🛂 Student visa — Ireland (from Egypt)
1
Get an unconditional offer from an Irish university
Apply via the university's international office. Non-EU deadline is typically December–February for September intake. You need an upper second class honours degree (2.1) or equivalent. Confirm the programme is on the ILEP (Interim List of Eligible Programmes) — mandatory for the student visa.
2
Prepare financial evidence — €10,000 accessible
Show immediate access to €10,000 for the first year (in addition to proof of tuition payment). This must be in a bank account that can be accessed in Ireland — not locked in a fixed-term deposit. Acceptable: personal bank statements, parental sponsor letter + their bank statements, scholarship letter. Ireland does not require a blocked account — funds just need to be demonstrably accessible.
⚠ Ireland tightened checks on large last-minute deposits in 2025–2026. Do not transfer a large sum just before applying — maintain a consistent balance and be ready to explain the source of funds.
3
Apply for student visa (D study visa) via VFS Global Egypt
All Egyptian nationals require a student visa. Apply at the Irish visa application centre (VFS Global, Cairo). Key documents: offer letter from Irish university, proof of tuition payment or scholarship, €10,000 financial evidence, passport, 2 passport photos, private health insurance, accommodation evidence. Visa fee: €60 (long-stay). Apply up to 3 months before the course start. Processing: 4–8 weeks.
4
Arrive and register with GNIB — within 90 days
Register with immigration (GNIB/IRP) to get your Stamp 2 student permission and IRP card. Fee: €300. Register at Burgh Quay in Dublin or at your local Garda station outside Dublin. You can work 20 hours/week during term and 40 hours/week during official holidays (June, July, August, and mid-December to mid-January).
5
Run CORU recognition in parallel (for Type B MSc students)
While studying in Ireland, submit your CORU recognition application for your Egyptian bachelor's degree. Being physically in Ireland makes document submission easier, allows you to respond quickly to CORU requests, and — if a Period of Adaptation is required — you can complete it during your studies without any separate visa.
🎓 After graduation — Stamp 1G (24 months)
After completing a Level 9 master's in Ireland, you are entitled to the Third Level Graduate Programme (Stamp 1G) — 24 months to find work and stay in Ireland, with full 40-hour/week work rights and no employer sponsorship required at application stage.
FeatureDetail
Duration24 months for master's (Level 9) — 12 months for bachelor's (Level 8)
Work rightsAny job, up to 40 hours/week — no employment permit needed during this period
Application timingApply after receiving final results; your Stamp 2 must still be valid when processed
Application fee€300 (IRP registration renewal)

Can you practise as a physio during Stamp 1G?Only if CORU registration is already complete. Stamp 1G gives work rights but not professional registration. You need both.

Important warningTime on Stamp 1G does NOT count toward Irish citizenship. You must move to CSEP → Stamp 4 to build the residency clock.
✓ The ideal Ireland sequence: Student visa → MSc (2 years, run CORU in parallel) → Stamp 1G (24 months) → get CORU registration + job offer → Critical Skills Employment Permit → Stamp 4 after 2 years → permanent residency pathway. Total from Egypt to Stamp 4 eligibility: approximately 5–6 years.

🛂 Student visa — Italy (from Egypt)
1
Pre-enrolment on Universitaly + Dichiarazione di Valore
Register on universitaly.it and submit pre-enrolment requests (typically April–July for following academic year). You also need a Dichiarazione di Valore (Declaration of Value) — a document from the Italian consulate in Cairo that validates your Egyptian degree within the Italian academic system. Request the DoV well in advance (can take 2–3 months). All Egyptian documents must go through the full consular legalisation chain first (Egyptian MFA → Italian consulate Cairo).
⚠ From January 2025, Italy requires in-person fingerprinting at the consulate for Type D visa applicants — book early as slots fill up.
2
Apply for Type D student visa via VFS Global Cairo
Documents required: Universitaly pre-enrolment confirmation, Dichiarazione di Valore, university acceptance letter, passport, passport photos, financial proof (sponsorship letter from parents + bank statements, or scholarship letter), accommodation evidence, Italian language certificate (if required by programme). Visa fee: ~€50–116. Processing: up to 90 days — apply early.
3
Arrive and apply for Permesso di Soggiorno per Studio — within 8 days
Within 8 days of arrival, submit the Permesso di Soggiorno application kit at a designated Poste Italiane office. Fee: ~€30 for the kit + €40–50 for the card. You can work 20 hours/week (max 1,040 hours/year) while studying. Register your address at the local Comune (Anagrafe) and apply for your Codice Fiscale (tax code) at the Agenzia delle Entrate — both are free and needed for many services.
4
Run DGPROF recognition in parallel (for your Egyptian bachelor's)
Being in Italy makes you physically present to respond to Ministry requests, attend any required tests, and enrol with the TSRM-PSTRP Order once the decree is issued. The Italian language course you take for your master's also prepares you for the mandatory language exam required before Order enrolment.
🎓 After graduation — Permesso per Attesa Occupazione (12 months)
After completing your Laurea Magistrale in Italy, you can apply for the Permesso di Soggiorno per Attesa Occupazione — a 12-month job-seeking permit (not renewable) that allows you to stay and work while searching for employment.
FeatureDetail
Duration12 months maximum — not renewable
Work rightsCan work full-time or part-time; internships also permitted (up to 12 months)
ApplicationApply at the local Questura (immigration police) before your student permit expires. Based on Article 39(5) of Legislative Decree 286/1998. University must be recognised by MIUR (Italian Ministry of University and Research).
ConversionIf you find a job, convert this to a Permesso di Lavoro Subordinato (work permit). Then apply for annual renewal as long as employed.
Critical constraintThis permit cannot be renewed. If you do not find work within 12 months, you must leave Italy. Processing is via the Questura — expect delays and bureaucracy.
Can you practise as a physio?Only if DGPROF recognition decree + language exam + TSRM-PSTRP Order enrolment are ALL complete. The job-seeking permit gives you legal stay but not professional registration.

🛂 Student visa — France (from Egypt)
1
Campus France "Études en France" procedure (mandatory for Egypt)
Egypt IS among countries where the Campus France procedure is mandatory. Create an account on the Études en France platform, upload your academic documents, pay the Campus France fee (~€40–60), and attend an interview at the Campus France office in Cairo if required. You receive a confirmation code used in your visa application. Start this 3–6 months before your planned start date.
2
Get accepted by a French university
Apply directly to university via the institution's portal (Parcoursup for some, direct application for postgraduate). For physiotherapy IFMK partial exemption, apply to the specific IFMK in your target region. All applications require French language proficiency proof: minimum DELF B2 for most programmes (C1 recommended for clinical/health programmes).
3
Apply for long-stay student visa (VLS-TS Étudiant) via TLScontact
Apply at the TLScontact centre (Cairo) for a VLS-TS Étudiant (long-stay visa worth a residence permit). Documents: Campus France confirmation, university acceptance, passport, financial proof (€615/month — no blocked account required for Egypt), accommodation proof, health insurance evidence, photos. Visa fee: ~€99. Apply 2–3 months before course start. Processing: 3–8 weeks. Egyptian nationals do NOT need to prove English proficiency for the visa itself.
✓ Good news for Egypt: no blocked account required and no English language test required. The main financial proof is €615/month accessible — a bank statement or parental sponsorship letter suffices.
4
Arrive and validate VLS-TS on ANEF portal — within 3 months
Validate your VLS-TS within 3 months of arriving in France via the ANEF portal (Administration Numérique pour les Étrangers en France). Pay the validation tax: €60 for students. This converts your visa into a residence title. Register with CPAM (health insurance) — students under 28 can register for free French student social security. Register your address at the mairie.
5
Run DREETS authorisation d'exercice process in parallel
Once in France, contact the regional DREETS where you will study and work. Being resident in France with a valid address + projet d'exercice (project to work in that region) substantially strengthens your application. If studying and completing clinical placements in France, your French placements count toward the 1,500-hour experience requirement.
🎓 After graduation — RÉCE permit / APS (12 months)
After completing a recognised master's degree from a French institution, you can apply for the Carte de Séjour — Recherche d'emploi ou Création d'entreprise (RÉCE), previously called APS. This gives 12 months to find work in France.
FeatureDetail
Duration12 months — not renewable
EligibilityMust have obtained a recognised master's degree (grade de master) from a French state-recognised institution; must hold a valid student permit at time of application
Work rightsCan work without restriction during this period (no separate work permit needed)
ApplicationApply online via ANEF portal before your student residence permit expires
Key benefit when transitioning to workWhen you get a qualifying job offer, the employer is EXEMPT from the labour market test (no need to prove no French/EU candidate was available), provided salary ≥ 1.5x SMIC and job is in your field of study
APS (bilateral agreement route)Egypt has not signed a specific bilateral migration agreement with France, so Egyptian graduates generally use the standard RÉCE route — not the APS. Confirm with the prefecture.
Practising as a physioRÉCE gives legal stay and work rights — but you still need the autorisation d'exercice from DREETS + Ordre registration to practise as a physiotherapist. These must be in place separately.

🛂 Student visa — Belgium (from Egypt)
1
Get unconditional admission from a Belgian university
Apply directly via the university. Non-EU deadline is typically February–March for September intake. Belgian universities typically require proof of language proficiency: Dutch B2 for Flemish universities (NT2 Staatsexamen), French B2 for Wallonia/Brussels. KU Leuven non-EU deadline is usually January. Check your target university's specific requirements.
2
Prove financial means — €1,062/month (2026–27 requirement)
Belgium has significantly increased its financial threshold for non-EU students for 2026–27: €1,062/month, meaning approximately €12,744 for a full year must be demonstrated. Options: (1) Sponsor (parent/guardian) letter + their bank statements showing sufficient funds; (2) Blocked account via Studely or Ready Study Go International — transfer equivalent to 12 months × €1,062 = €12,744 into a blocked account in your name; (3) Employment contract covering the required amount; (4) Scholarship letter from a recognised body.
⚠ The 2026–27 Belgian requirement (€1,062/month) is the highest of the four countries and applies for each year of study. Plan accordingly.
3
Apply for Type D long-stay student visa at Belgian embassy
Apply at the Belgian embassy or consulate serving Egypt. Documents: unconditional admission letter, proof of financial means, police clearance (criminal record certificate), medical certificate (for applicants over 21), passport photos, passport. Visa fee: €210 for long-stay. Processing: several weeks to 3+ months — apply early. All documents in languages other than Dutch, French, German, or English must be translated by a sworn translator.
4
Arrive and register at commune — within 8 working days
Register at your local commune's Aliens Department. Receive an A-card (residence permit). Renew annually — Belgian immigration assesses study progress each year (must earn 60 credits/year for master's students; failure to progress can result in permit refusal). Register with a ziekenfonds/mutuelle for health insurance. Open a Belgian bank account. You can work 20 hours/week during term and unlimited hours during holidays.
5
Begin NARIC + Departement Zorg / FWB recognition during studies
Apply for NARIC academic equivalence for your Egyptian bachelor's. Belgian universities and recognition bodies are familiar with international applicants. Clinical placements during your Belgian master's provide documented experience in Belgium, which significantly strengthens your recognition application. The recognition process and the master's can run in parallel.

Month by month

Realistic timeline — study to full practice (Ireland example)

The following timeline uses Ireland as the example (Type B specialist MSc + CORU recognition running in parallel). Timelines for Italy, France and Belgium are broadly similar but with language learning added.

Month −18 to −12 — Egypt: foundations
Start language study (French/Italian/Dutch if not Ireland). Gather all documents from university. Begin Egyptian legalisation chain for degree + transcripts. Sit IELTS or local language exam. Research programmes and application deadlines. Build personal statement and get reference letters.
Month −12 to −6 — Apply to universities
Submit applications (non-EU deadlines: Dec–Feb for Ireland/Belgium; open for Italy via Universitaly April–July; France varies). Arrange financial proof. If going to France: register on Campus France / Études en France platform immediately. Await conditional offers. Apply for scholarships (VLIR-UOS, Eiffel, Government of Ireland — most have deadlines 12+ months before programme start).
Month −6 to −3 — Offer accepted + visa application
Receive unconditional offer. Apply for student visa at the relevant embassy/VFS/TLScontact in Cairo. Ireland: €60 D visa via VFS. Italy: €50–116 D visa via VFS (+ fingerprinting appointment). France: €99 VLS-TS via TLScontact. Belgium: €210 D visa at Belgian embassy. Apply 3+ months before start date. Arrange accommodation (university dorms fill fast — apply immediately).
Month 0 — Arrival
Arrive, settle in. Within 8 days (Italy, Belgium) or 90 days (Ireland, France): register with immigration and get your residence card/IRP. Register with health insurance. Open bank account. Get local SIM. Start orientation week at university. Begin looking for part-time work opportunities in healthcare settings.
Month 1–3 — Submit CORU/DGPROF/NARIC recognition in parallel
Don't wait until you graduate. Submit your recognition application for your Egyptian bachelor's degree while you are studying. Being physically present means faster document exchange, ability to respond to queries, and — critically — if a Period of Adaptation is required, it can be done during your studies without a separate visa problem.
Year 1–2 — Study, work part-time, build networks
Complete your master's. Work part-time (20h/week during term, 40h/week holidays) to earn €10,000–15,000/yr to offset costs. Build professional networks — attend ISCP (Ireland), AIFI (Italy), or your national physiotherapy association events. Complete any Period of Adaptation during clinical placements in your programme if recognition requires it.
Month 24 — Graduate + apply for post-study stay
Ireland: Apply for Stamp 1G immediately after final results — 24 months. Italy: Apply at Questura for Permesso Attesa Occupazione — 12 months. France: Apply via ANEF for RÉCE permit — 12 months. Belgium: Apply at commune for Orientation Year — 12 months. Ensure your student permit is still valid when you apply.
Month 24–36 — Full-time job search + registration
By this point, aim to have: (1) Recognition decision from CORU/DGPROF/Departement Zorg; (2) Language exam passed (Italy/France/Belgium); (3) Professional order enrolment completed; (4) Job offer confirmed. With all of these, you are a fully registered, practising physiotherapist with a legal right to work in the country. Employer applies for work permit; you transition from post-study status to full employment.
Year 3–5 — Building long-term residency
Ireland: CSEP → Stamp 4 after 2 years → permanent residency pathway. Italy: Carta di Soggiorno after 5 years. France: Carte de Résident after 5 years of regular residence. Belgium: EU long-term residence permit after 5 years. All four countries provide a route to permanent residency and, eventually, citizenship for those who remain employed in their profession.
